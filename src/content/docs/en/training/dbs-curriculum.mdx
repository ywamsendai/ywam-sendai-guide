The Discipleship Bible School follows a specific weekly rhythm, covering different groups of books each week.

<Steps>
1.  **Week 1: Worldview & Origins** *Books:* Genesis, Exodus, Job  
    *Focus:* The Creation story, the Fall of Man, and God's original intent for humanity. We address the problem of evil through the book of Job.

2.  **Week 2: Pentateuch** *Books:* Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy  
    *Focus:* The Mosaic Covenant, the wilderness wanderings, and the principles of the Law applied to modern life.

3.  **Week 3: Tribal History** *Books:* Joshua, Judges, Ruth, Psalms  
    *Focus:* Conquering the Promised Land. We study the importance of faithfulness versus cultural pressure and engage with God through the Psalms.

4.  **Week 4: The Kingdom of David** *Books:* 1 & 2 Samuel, 1 Chronicles, Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Song of Songs  
    *Focus:* The transition to monarchy and the character of David. This week introduces "Wisdom Literature" and Hebrew poetry.

5.  **Week 5: The Kings of Israel & Judah** *Books:* 1 & 2 Kings, 2 Chronicles  
    *Focus:* The split of the Kingdom and the rise of the first world empires (Assyria, Babylon, Persia). We study the lives of Elijah and Elisha.

6.  **Week 6: The Early Prophets** *Books:* Isaiah, Hosea, Joel, Amos, Jonah, Micah, Nahum  
    *Focus:* Understanding prophetic literature. Themes include social justice (Amos), God’s father heart (Hosea), and the mission to the Gentiles (Jonah).

7.  **Week 7: The Late Prophets & Exile** *Books:* Jeremiah, Lamentations, Ezekiel, Daniel, Obadiah, Zephaniah, Habakkuk  
    *Focus:* Israel in exile. We study the promise of the New Covenant and how to remain a witness in a pagan or secular culture.

8.  **Week 8: Post-Exilic & Inter-Testament** *Books:* Ezra, Nehemiah, Esther, Haggai, Zechariah, Malachi  
    *Focus:* The return to Jerusalem and rebuilding. We bridge the 400-year gap between the Old and New Testaments.

9.  **Week 9: The Life of Jesus** *Books:* Matthew, Mark, Luke  
    *Focus:* The arrival of the Kingdom of God. We compare the Synoptic Gospels and study the parables of Jesus.

10. **Week 10: The Early Church** *Books:* Acts, Hebrews, James, 1 & 2 Peter, Jude  
    *Focus:* The birth of the Church at Pentecost and the spread of the Gospel. Introduction to the Epistles (letters).

11. **Week 11: The Pauline Epistles** *Books:* Romans, 1 & 2 Corinthians, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, 1 & 2 Thessalonians, 1 & 2 Timothy, Titus, Philemon  
    *Focus:* Paul’s missionary journeys and his influence on theology (grace, justification, spiritual warfare, and identity).

12. **Week 12: The Johannine Writings** *Books:* John, 1-3 John, Revelation  
    *Focus:* The perspective of the Apostle John. We conclude with "Apocalyptic Literature" and the study of the Second Coming in Revelation.
</Steps>
