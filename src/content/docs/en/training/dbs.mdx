The DBS is a 12-week Bible immersion course. After finishing your DTS, the DBS provides the opportunity to read through all 66 books of the Bible, discovering the chronological story of God’s redemptive work.

---

## 1. The Inductive Approach
We don't just read the Bible; we learn to *observe* it.
* **Observation:** What does the text actually say?
* **Interpretation:** What did it mean to the original audience?
* **Application:** How does this truth change my life in Sendai today?

---

## 2. Key Components
* **Chronological Reading:** Understanding the Bible as one unified story from Genesis to Revelation.
* **Small Group Tables:** Processing the week's reading in a community of discovery.
* **Worldview:** Identifying and aligning our perspectives with the Biblical narrative.

---

## 3. Weekly Commitments
The DBS is intense but rewarding. Each week includes:
* **Bible Reading:** Significant personal time dedicated to reading the assigned books.
* **Lectures:** Insights from speakers on historical context and themes.
* **Outreach (Optional):** Many DBS courses include a short outreach to share the "Story of God" with others.

<Aside type="tip" title="Prerequisite">
Successful completion of a Discipleship Training School (DTS) is required for all DBS students.
</Aside>
