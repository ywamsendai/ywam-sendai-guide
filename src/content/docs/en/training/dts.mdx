The DTS is our foundational "Live-Learn" experience. It is more than a course; it is an invitation to be transformed by the character of God and to join His mission in the world.

## 1. The Two Phases
The DTS is divided into two distinct chapters of growth.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Lecture Phase (12 Weeks)" icon="open-book">
    A time of "Deep Roots." Through weekly teaching, small groups, and communal living, we focus on hearing God's voice and character transformation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Field Assignment (8-12 Weeks)" icon="rocket">
    A time of "Fruitfulness." We head into the harvest field—often within Tohoku or neighboring nations—to apply what we learned through service and evangelism.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## 2. Core Curriculum Units
Based on the UofN curriculum standards, we explore:
* **God’s Nature & Character:** Understanding who God is and how He relates to us.
* **God’s Intent for Individuals & Nations:** Discovering our purpose in His story.
* **The Person & Work of Jesus:** Centering our lives on the Gospel.
* **The Holy Spirit:** Learning to walk in His power and guidance.
* **Great Commission:** Our role in reaching the lost and influencing society.

---

## 3. The Weekly Rhythm
As a "Modern Monastic" school, your schedule is a blend of study and service:
* **Lectures:** 12–16 hours of bilingual teaching.
* **Intercession & Worship:** 3+ hours of focused corporate prayer.
* **One-on-Ones:** Regular mentorship with a staff member.
* **Work Duty:** 2 hours daily of serving the house (Kitchen, Grounds, Hospitality).

<Aside type="note" title="Prerequisite">
The DTS is the prerequisite for all other UofN courses and for serving as long-term staff in YWAM.
</Aside>
