# Digital Stewardship

Our digital tools are servants, not masters. We use them to foster bilingual unity while protecting the "monastic science" of deep rest and focus.

---

## 1. The Digital Sabbath (Saturday)
Aligning with ancient tradition, we observe our primary Digital Fast on **Saturday (The Sabbath)**. 

* **The Goal:** Total withdrawal from ministry-related digital noise to foster interior silence and personal recovery.
* **No Pings:** Do not send Discord pings or ministry emails from **Friday Sunset to Sunday Morning**. 
* **Sundays:** Sunday is a day for communal worship and church service. While digital use is permitted, we prioritize being "fully present" with the local Japanese church over digital engagement.

---

## 2. Digital Curfew & Sleep Hygiene
High-quality sleep is a prerequisite for high-level ministry. To protect the community’s circadian rhythms, we observe a digital curfew.

* **21:00 Curfew:** Refrain from sending non-emergency Discord messages after **21:00**. 
* **Blue Light & Focus:** We encourage staff to disconnect from screens at least 60 minutes before sleep to ensure cognitive recovery for the next day's "Deep Work."

---

## 3. Discord: Our Virtual Hub
To prevent notification fatigue, we follow these protocols:

* **Asynchronous Expectations:** Reply to non-emergency pings within **24 hours** (Monday–Friday). 
* **Status Indicators:** Use your status to signal your current rhythm (e.g., 🟢 Available, ⛔ Deep Work, 🌙 Sabbath).
* **Thread Usage:** Use Discord "Threads" for specific technical or school-related discussions to keep main channels clear and scannable.

---

## 4. GitHub: Our Source of Truth
* **Documentation Rule:** A policy isn't "official" until the Pull Request is merged into this Guide.
* **Contribution:** All staff are encouraged to suggest edits via GitHub to ensure the Guide reflects our shared experience in Sendai.
