YWAM Sendai operates as a local ministry hub under the umbrella of the **YWAM Japan Religious Corporation (Shūkyō Hōjin)**. This national legal structure provides us with the standing required to operate as a Christian mission in Japan.

---

## 1. The Shūkyō Hōjin Umbrella
Being part of the National Religious Corporation means we share in a collective legal identity with other YWAM bases across Japan.

* **National Oversight:** While Sendai has local leadership, we align with the national standards and reporting requirements of YWAM Japan.
* **Non-Profit Status:** As a religious corporation, our primary activities are recognized by the Japanese government as non-profit religious and educational work.
* **Asset Stewardship:** Facilities and major assets are stewarded in accordance with the regulations set by the national board.

---

## 2. Visas & Religious Activities
The Shūkyō Hōjin acts as the legal sponsor for staff "Religious Activities" visas.
* **Sponsorship:** Our status under the national corporation allows us to facilitate the presence of international staff in Tohoku.
* **Compliance:** It is vital that staff activities remain focused on religious, educational, and charitable work to maintain the integrity of our visa sponsorship.

---

## 3. Local Financial Accountability
While we are legally under the national umbrella, YWAM Sendai maintains local financial transparency:
* **Internal Audits:** We maintain clear records of all local income and expenses, which are reported to the national office as required.
* **Integrity:** We treat our paperwork and local government interactions as a primary part of our [Public Witness](../../foundations/vision-values).

<Aside type="note" title="Future Developments">
As the Sendai community grows, we continue to evaluate the most effective legal structures to support the expansion of our work in the Tohoku region.
</Aside>
