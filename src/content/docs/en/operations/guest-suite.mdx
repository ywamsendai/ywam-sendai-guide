Our guest rooms are not just places to sleep; they are "cities of refuge" for travelers and weary ministers. We aim for a standard of cleanliness and warmth that reflects the hospitality of Christ.

---

## 1. The Arrival Experience
The first 15 minutes of a guest’s arrival define their sense of safety and belonging.

* **The Greeting:** If possible, meet the guest at the nearest station or the Hub entrance. A warm, bilingual greeting immediately breaks the ice.
* **The Orientation:** Give a brief tour of the Hub. Highlight the coffee station, the [Common Table](../../hospitality/common-table), and explain the [Digital Curfew](../../operations/digital) gently.
* **The Welcome Pack:** Ensure a physical "Welcome Guide" is on the desk, including the Wi-Fi password, local map of Aoba-ku, and a handwritten note.

---

## 2. Room Preparation Standards
We follow a "Hotel Standard" for cleanliness to ensure guests feel truly cared for.

<Steps>
1.  **Linens:** Use fresh, high-quality cotton sheets and towels. Ensure they are professionally laundered or dried in the sun.
2.  **Climate Control:** Pre-set the AC or heater to a comfortable temperature (22°C) 30 minutes before arrival.
3.  **Hydration:** Provide a glass bottle of filtered water and two clean glasses in the room.
4.  **Aesthetics:** Ensure the room is clutter-free. A single sprig of greenery or a local flower in a vase is a simple but powerful touch.
</Steps>

---

## 3. Hosting Mindset
Hosting is a shared community responsibility.
* **Visibility:** When a guest is in the Hub, make an effort to acknowledge them. A simple "Good morning" or "Welcome to Sendai" goes a long way.
* **Inclusion:** Invite them to the Common Table, but respect their need for "Selah" (rest) if they choose to stay in their room.
* **Departure:** Always see a guest to the door. In Japanese culture, watching a guest until they are out of sight is a sign of deep respect.

<Aside type="tip" title="Tohoku Seasonal Care">
In winter, provide extra blankets and a heat pack (*kairo*). In summer, ensure a fan is available and provide a cold towel (*oshibori*) upon arrival.
</Aside>
