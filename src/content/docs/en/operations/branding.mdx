Our communication is an extension of our hospitality. We aim for clarity, beauty, and humility in everything we publish, from Instagram posts to official newsletters.

---

## 1. Our Voice & Tone
We avoid "Christianese" and hype. Instead, we speak with:
* **Rootedness:** Our language reflects our commitment to Tohoku and our historical foundations.
* **Clarity:** We use simple, direct language that is accessible to both English and Japanese speakers.
* **Hopeful Realism:** We share the honest challenges of mission in Japan without losing sight of God's faithfulness.

---

## 2. Visual Identity
To maintain a cohesive look, we follow these core brand standards:

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="The Logo" icon="pencil">
    Always use the high-resolution YWAM Sendai logo. Do not stretch, recolor, or alter the proportions. 
  </Card>
  <Card title="Typography" icon="font">
    Our primary fonts are clean and sans-serif for digital use (like the ones used in this Guide) and elegant serif fonts for formal print.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Photography" icon="camera">
    Prioritize "candid" photography that shows authentic community life. Avoid stock photos or overly "staged" shots.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## 3. Social Media & Newsletters
* **Bilingual Standard:** All public-facing posts should ideally be bilingual (English/Japanese).
* **Privacy & Consent:** Always ask for permission before posting photos of local Japanese friends, church members, or students. 
* **Frequency:** We prioritize **Quality over Quantity**. One thoughtful update per month is better than daily posts without depth.

---

## 4. Newsletter Protocol
For those writing personal or base newsletters:
1. **The "Mother" Test:** Write in a way that your mother would understand, but also in a way that respects a Japanese neighbor reading a translation.
2. **Call to Action:** Always include a clear way for people to pray or get involved, rather than just "reporting" facts.

<Aside type="tip" title="Digital Stewardship">
Remember that our digital presence is often the first "room" people enter when visiting our community. Keep it clean, welcoming, and truthful.
</Aside>
