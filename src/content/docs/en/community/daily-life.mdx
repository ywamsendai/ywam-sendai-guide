To love the people of Sendai is to respect the social harmony (*Wa*) of our neighborhood. Whether you are here for a month or a year, your daily habits reflect the character of our community to our neighbors in Aoba-ku.

---

## 1. Neighborly Etiquette
In Japan, small gestures build long-term trust. We follow these rules not out of legalism, but as an act of incarnational love.

*   **The Science of Garbage:** Waste management is strict. Use only official Sendai City bags for "Burnable" and "Non-burnable" waste. Follow the sorting guide in the Hub kitchen exactly—errors result in garbage being left on the street, which burdens our neighbors.
*   **Noise Levels:** Sound travels easily through Japanese walls. Please keep voices low when walking to and from the Hub, especially after the 21:00 digital curfew.
*   **Greetings (*Aisatsu*):** A polite "Ohayō gozaimasu" (Morning) or "Konnichiwa" (Day) with a slight bow builds vital rapport with those living around us.
*   **Public Transit:** On the subway or bus, keep conversation volume to a minimum and avoid talking on the phone.

---

## 2. Getting Around Sendai
Sendai is a highly walkable city, designed around the beautiful "City of Trees" aesthetic.

*   **IC Cards (Suica/Icoca):** These rechargeable cards are essential for the Subway and City Bus. You can also use them at most *conbini* (convenience stores).
*   **Rental Cycles:** While personal bike registration is reserved for long-term residents, you can use **DATE BIKE** (Sendai’s bike-share). These red electric bikes are dotted around the city and are perfect for quick trips.
*   **Walking:** Always be mindful of pedestrian flow, especially on busy streets like Jozenji-dori.

---

## 3. Money & Practicalities
*   **Cash is King:** While digital payments are growing, many local Aoba-ku shops still prefer cash. Always carry some Yen.
*   **ATMs:** Use **7-Eleven (7-Bank)** or **Japan Post** ATMs for the best compatibility with international cards. We recommend **Wise** or **Revolut** for exchange rates.
*   **Conbini Culture:** Convenience stores are your 24/7 resource for printing documents, shipping packages, or grabbing a quick healthy snack.

---

## 4. Healthcare & Safety
*   **Emergencies:** In a medical emergency, dial **119** and say **"Kyūkyū"** (Ambulance). Notify staff immediately.
*   **Local Clinics:** There are several excellent clinics within walking distance of the Hub.
*   **Abuse Prevention:** We maintain a zero-tolerance policy. If you witness or experience anything of concern, contact leadership immediately via our confidential channels.
