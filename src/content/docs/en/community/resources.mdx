To maintain a common "mental vocabulary," we recommend the following resources. These are selected to align with our focus on longevity, cultural intelligence, and spiritual depth.

---

## 1. The Language Library
We prioritize tools that utilize **Spaced Repetition (SRS)** and **Comprehensible Input**.

* **Anki:** The gold standard for vocabulary retention. We recommend the "Core 2k/6k" decks.
* **WaniKani:** An excellent gamified system for mastering Kanji and radicals.
* **Satori Reader:** Perfect for intermediate learners to bridge the gap between textbooks and real Japanese literature.
* **Dictionaries:** We recommend the **Shirabe Jisho** app (iOS/Android) for quick, offline lookups.

---

## 2. Understanding the Japanese Mind
Mission in Tohoku requires a deep "exegeting" of the local culture and history.

* **"The Chrysanthemum and the Sword" by Ruth Benedict:** Though dated, it remains a foundational text for understanding the "shame vs. guilt" cultural framework.
* **"Silence" by Shusaku Endo:** A vital (though heavy) historical novel about the complexities of faith and "the swamp of Japan."
* **"Japan: The Paradox of Harmony" by Keiko Hirata:** Insightful for understanding the *Honne* (true feeling) and *Tatemae* (public face) dynamics.

---

## 3. Spiritual & YWAM Foundation
These books define our "DNA" as a movement and our approach to the contemplative-active life.

* **"Is That Really You, God?" by Loren Cunningham:** The foundational story of YWAM and hearing God's voice.
* **"The Spirit of the Disciplines" by Dallas Willard:** Vital for understanding how we are formed into the image of Christ.
* **"A Guide to Living in the Truth" by Michael Casey:** A Cistercian monk’s look at humility—highly relevant for the long, quiet work in Tohoku.

---

## 4. Longevity & Human Energy
In keeping with our [30+5 Work Rhythm](../../community/rhythms-of-life), we look to these resources for biological stewardship.

* **"How Not to Die" by Dr. Michael Greger:** A comprehensive, evidence-based guide on how a whole-food, plant-based diet can prevent and reverse disease, supporting long-term mission viability.
* **"Deep Work" by Cal Newport:** The definitive guide on how to focus in a distracted world.
* **"Why We Sleep" by Matthew Walker:** Essential reading to understand the biological necessity of our [Digital Curfew](../../operations/digital).
