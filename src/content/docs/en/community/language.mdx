# The Language of the Heart

To love Sendai is to speak its language. We don't study just to pass exams; we study to bridge the gap between cultures and to love our neighbors with excellence.

---

## 1. Useful Phrases for Life & Mission
Use these phrases to navigate daily life and build rapport with our neighbors in Aoba-ku.

| Situation | English | Japanese (Polite) | Reading (Romaji) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Greeting** | Good morning | おはようございます | Ohayō gozaimasu |
| **Greeting** | Hello / Good day | こんにちは | Konnichiwa |
| **Gratitude** | Thank you very much | ありがとうございます | Arigatō gozaimasu |
| **Shopping** | I'll take this, please | これをください | Kore o kudasai |
| **Request** | One more time, slowly | もう一度、ゆっくり | Mō ichido, yukkuri |
| **Local Flavor** | It feels "not quite right" | いずい | Izui (Sendai-ben) |
| **Community** | Let's eat! (Before meals) | いただきます | Itadakimasu |

---

## 2. The House Language Policy
We treat the Hub as a "Growth Environment" where we prioritize Japanese for the sake of hospitality and learning.

* **The Common Table:** We encourage lunch conversations to be in Japanese. This allows beginners to practice "active listening" and intermediates to build confidence.
* **The Rule of Grace:** We value the *effort* to speak broken Japanese over the *comfort* of perfect English. Don't be afraid to make mistakes—they are the price of progress.
* **Bilingual Life:** You will notice our documentation and signage are bilingual. This is to help you absorb the "technical" vocabulary of our mission.

---

## 3. Survival Immersion
Language is a social act. We encourage everyone to find "Third Spaces" in the city:
* **Local Shops:** Practice your "Survival Japanese" at the grocery store or the neighborhood bakery.
* **Sendai-ben:** Learn a few local phrases! Using a word like *Izui* (local dialect for "uncomfortable/not quite right") shows our neighbors that we are committed to *their* specific culture.

<Aside type="tip" title="Daily Goal">
Take one new phrase you heard in the morning and try to use it with a local person before the sun goes down.
</Aside>
