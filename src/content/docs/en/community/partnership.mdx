Raising support is not about "asking for money"; it is about inviting others into what God is doing in Japan. We believe that if God guides, He provides.

---

## 1. Core Principles
* **God is the Source:** People are the channel, but God is the provider. 
* **Relational, Not Transactional:** You are inviting partners, not looking for donors.
* **Be Active, Not Passive:** Faith requires action. We step out in the application process as an act of faith that the funds will follow.

---

## 2. Practical Steps to Start
<Steps>
1. **The Contact List:** List everyone you know—family, church, friends, and coworkers. 
2. **Draft Your Story:** Write a 1-2 page letter or create a digital pamphlet. Include:
    * **The "Why":** Why Japan? Why now?
    * **The "What":** What will you be doing (DTS, Staffing, Outreach)?
    * **The "How":** Clear instructions on how to give.
3. **The Face-to-Face:** Whenever possible, meet with people or your Pastor. Share your heart first, then the need.
</Steps>

## 3. Recommended Tools for Receiving Funds
Supporters appreciate methods that are familiar and provide tax benefits where possible.

  <Card title="USA: EquipNet" icon="external">

  </Card>
  <Card title="UK: Stewardship" icon="external">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Global: Wise / PayPal" icon="external">
    Best for friends who do not require a tax receipt.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## 4. Communication Rhythms
* **Update Frequently:** Use social media or a newsletter (Mailchimp/Substack) to share "God-stories," not just "Money-needs."
* **The 100-Friend Goal:** Ask 100 people to give a small, manageable amount (e.g., ¥1,000 or $10/mo).
* **Gratitude:** Personally thank every supporter. A handwritten card or a video message goes a long way.
