In Sendai, the table is an altar. It is where we transition from our morning of "Deep Work" into an afternoon of community and service. Our goal is to create a space that is nutritionally dense, spiritually centering, and linguistically inclusive.

---

## 1. The Midday Liturgy
We don't just "eat lunch"; we observe a brief rhythm to re-center our hearts mid-day.

* **12:25 – The Gathering:** Everyone arrives at the Hub. Help with final plating or water pouring.
* **12:30 – The Grace:** A designated member leads a brief prayer or a short liturgical reading (bilingual).
* **The Meal:** We prioritize slow eating and meaningful conversation.
* **The Closing:** We end with a brief "Thank you for the meal" (*Gochisōsama*) and a quick update on the afternoon schedule.

---

## 2. Bilingual Inclusivity
The table is our primary classroom for "Practical Bilingualism."
* **The Seat Map:** Staff should intentionally sit between students or guests to facilitate translation. 
* **The Rule of Inclusion:** If more than two people at a table do not speak a language being used, the conversation should shift or be summarized in the other language.
* **Active Learning:** Use this time to practice basic Japanese/English food-related phrases.

---

## 3. Nutritional Stewardship
Aligned with our [Longevity Focus](../../foundations/vision-values), we aim for:
* **Whole Foods:** Prioritizing local, seasonal Miyagi produce.
* **Additive-Free:** Using traditional Japanese seasonings (real miso, shoyu, and mirin) without hidden additives.
* **Clean-Up:** Many hands make light work. We follow a "Wash your own, dry for others" protocol to keep the kitchen functional.
* **Dietary Stewardship:** We share life around the table. If you have specific allergies or dietary restrictions, please inform us during the [Onboarding Process](../../operations/onboarding-philosophy). While we strive for a clean, whole-food menu, knowing your needs helps us prepare the table with excellence.

<Aside type="note" title="Table Hosting">
The "Table Host" (usually a staff member) is responsible for ensuring guests feel included in the conversation and that the bilingual flow is maintained.
</Aside>
