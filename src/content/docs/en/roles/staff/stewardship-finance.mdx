Long-term residency in Japan is a stewardship of both your calling and your legal standing. As "self-employed" (*kojin jigyo*) missionaries, you are responsible for navigating these systems with excellence.

---

## 1. The "Big Four" Obligations
All staff residing in Sendai must manage these four pillars at the **Aoba-ku Ward Office**.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="National Health Insurance (NHI)" icon="heart">
    **Kokumin Kenko Hoken.** Mandatory. Premiums are based on your previous year's Japanese income.
  </Card>
  <Card title="National Pension" icon="warning">
    **Kokumin Nenkin.** Mandatory flat-rate contribution. Check for "Social Security Agreements" with your home country to avoid double-paying.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Income Tax" icon="pencil">
    **Shotokuzei.** Annual filing (*Kakutei Shinkoku*) is required between Feb 16 – March 15. You must report global income.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resident Tax" icon="external">
    **Juminzei.** Local tax billed a year in arrears. Save ahead for your second year in Japan!
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## 2. Residency: The Tokutei Zairyū Card
The **Tokutei Zairyū Card (Integrated Residence Card)** is your most vital document.
* **Updates:** Report changes in address or visa status within **14 days**.
* **Tax Filing:** It contains your digital certificate for e-Tax. Never share the PIN.

---

## 3. Budgeting & Support Goals (2026)
To sustain ministry in Tohoku, aim for these monthly support targets:
* **Single:** ¥140k - ¥170k
* **Couple:** ¥240k - ¥280k
* **Family:** ¥320k - ¥450k+

---

## 4. Banking & Tax Filing
* **Yucho Bank:** Standard for local bill payments.
* **Tracking:** Keep a monthly log of all support (in JPY) and ministry-related expenses for your tax return.

<Aside type="danger" title="U.S. Citizens">
You must file in both the U.S. and Japan. Seek professional advice on the "Foreign Earned Income Exclusion" to prevent double taxation.
</Aside>
