In YWAM Sendai, we believe that we are "transformed into His likeness" within the context of relationship. Mentorship is not about management; it is about **spiritual direction**—helping another person recognize and respond to God’s presence in their life.

---

## 1. The Structure of a 1-on-1
While every relationship is unique, we recommend a 60-minute weekly or bi-weekly cadence for staff and students. A healthy session usually follows a simple three-part flow:

1.  **Look Back (Connection):** How have you been since we last met? What were the high and low points of your week?
2.  **Look In (Formation):** What is God saying to you through the Word or your circumstances? How are you responding to Him?
3.  **Look Ahead (Action):** What is one practical step you can take this week to align more with Christ’s character?

---

## 2. Core Areas of Focus
We use the following pillars to ensure holistic growth:

* **Spiritual Health:** Are you maintaining your [Personal Rhythms](../../../community/rhythms-of-life)? How is your intimacy with God?
* **Relational Health:** Is there any unresolved conflict in the community or with your family?
* **Physical Stewardship:** Are you sleeping well? How is your energy management?
* **Cultural Integration:** How is your heart toward Japan? Are you finding joy in the language and the people?

---

## 3. The Role of the Mentor
Your goal is not to have all the answers, but to ask the right questions. 

* **Active Listening:** Listen more than you speak. Allow for silence; often, the Holy Spirit speaks in the pauses.
* **Confidentiality:** Mentorship requires a high level of trust. Keep what is shared private, unless it involves a safety risk or a violation of our [Safeguarding Policy](../../../operations/safety).
* **Modeling Humility:** Be willing to share your own struggles and what God is teaching you.

<Aside type="tip" title="Spiritual Direction">
Remember: You are a "midwife" for what God is already birthing in them. Focus on helping them hear His voice rather than giving your own advice.
</Aside>
