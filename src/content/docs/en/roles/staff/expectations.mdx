Staff members carry the responsibility of maintaining the "trellis" of our community. This page outlines the operational requirements exclusive to the long-term team.

---

## 1. The 30+5 Work Model
To support long-term mission viability, staff manage their time through a professional **30+5** structure:

* **30 Hours (Core Ministry):** School staffing, administrative duties, outreach coordination, and Hub operations.
* **5 Hours (Development):** Dedicated time for **Personal Growth**. This is not for email or admin; use it for Japanese language study, reading from the [Recommended Resources](../../../community/resources), or skill training.

---

## 2. Operational Anchors
As the engine of the base, staff are required to attend these strategic meetings:

* **Tuesday: Core Alignment (90 mins):** Our weekly engine for base logistics, financial updates, and strategic planning.
* **Weekly 1-on-1s:** Staff participate in spiritual direction and mentorship, both as givers (to students) and receivers (with leadership).

---

## 3. Annual Leave & Sabbaticals
Staff are responsible for the long-term stewardship of their souls through planned leave:

| Type | Duration | Frequency | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Deep Rest** | 10 Days | Yearly | Mandatory total disconnection/Logout. |
| **Furlough** | 3 Weeks | Yearly | Support raising and home-country relations. |
| **Sabbatical** | 3 Months | Every 5-7 Years | Deep recovery, study, and re-entry prep. |

---

## 4. Culture Carrying
Staff are expected to model the **Sendai Standard** in the following areas:
1.  **Bilingualism:** Proactively facilitating a bilingual environment at the Common Table.
2.  **Mentorship:** Walking with students through their formation journey.
3.  **Governance:** Understanding and upholding the [Religious Corporation](../../../operations/legal) standards.

<Aside type="caution" title="Proactive Communication">
We expect staff to be transparent about their capacity. If the 30+5 rhythm is becoming unsustainable, it is your responsibility to raise this in Core Alignment.
</Aside>
