For long-term staff, language is a tool of stewardship. We set **JLPT N2** as our standard to ensure legal credibility and the ability to provide deep emotional or spiritual care without a translator.

---

## 1. The 2+1 Method
Based on cognitive science, we recommend this structure for your weekly study blocks (16 hours for Years 1-2; 8 hours for Year 3+).

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="2 Hours: Deep Study" icon="pencil">
    Focused, "Cold" study (Kanji, Grammar, Textbooks). Best done in the morning during your peak cognitive window.
  </Card>
  <Card title="1 Hour: Interleaved Review" icon="refresh">
    Short bursts of review (Anki, podcasts, shadowing) mixed with other activities to strengthen long-term recall.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## 2. The Interleaving Strategy
Research shows that mixing different topics (Kanji + Listening + Speaking) in one session is superior to "blocked" study.

<Steps>
1.  **Switch Topics:** Every 30 minutes, switch from a passive task (reading) to an active task (writing/speaking).
2.  **Spaced Repetition:** Use SRS tools like Anki daily to maintain your "Mental Library."
3.  **Active Interleaving:** Apply a new grammar point immediately during staff meetings or ministry times.
</Steps>
