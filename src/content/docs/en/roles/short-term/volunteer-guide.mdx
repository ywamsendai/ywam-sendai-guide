# Welcome to Sendai

We are so grateful you have chosen to serve with us. Whether you are here for a week or a month, you are a vital part of our community. 

---

## 1. The Goal: Servant Presence
Our primary goal as volunteers is to support the long-term work already happening in Sendai. We serve by "washing the feet" of the long-term staff and the local church.

* **Flexibility:** Plans in Japan can change quickly. A "willing heart" is your most valuable tool.
* **Observation:** Spend your first few days observing the [Rhythms of Life](../../../community/rhythms-of-life) and how staff interact with the culture.

---

## 2. Cultural "Quick-Start"
Tohoku is a region of deep respect and quiet dignity. Please keep these three things in mind:

1.  **The Shoe Rule:** Never wear outdoor shoes inside the Hub or a Japanese home. Use the provided slippers or your socks.
2.  **Voice Levels:** In public (trains, cafes, and even the Hub hallways), we keep our voices low to honor the peace of those around us.
3.  **The Gift of Punctuality:** In Japan, "on time" means 5 minutes early. 

---

## 3. Practicalities
* **Communication:** We use Discord for base-wide updates. You will be given a "Guest" invite upon arrival.
* **Internet:** High-speed Wi-Fi is available at the Hub, but please refrain from high-bandwidth activities (streaming/gaming) during "Deep Work" hours.
* **Waste Management:** Recycling in Sendai is a science. Please follow the signs on the bins carefully—mixing plastics and paper is a significant burden for the staff to fix!

<Aside type="tip" title="Explore Aoba-ku">
During your time off, we encourage you to walk the neighborhood. There are beautiful small shrines and local coffee shops nearby that are perfect for prayer and reflection.
</Aside>
