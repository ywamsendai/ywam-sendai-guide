Welcome to Sendai! You are here to put your training into practice through service and evangelism. To make your time successful and honor our local community, we ask you to adopt the following standards.

---

## 1. Living in Community
* **Punctuality:** In Japan, "on time" means 5 minutes early. Please be at all meetings and ministry departures ahead of schedule.
* **Living Conditions:** Space in Japan is a premium. We share close quarters and follow strict cleaning protocols to keep the Hub functional for everyone.
* **Communication:** We use Discord for team updates. Please check the relevant channels daily for schedule changes.

---

## 2. Cultural Etiquette
* **The Shoe Policy:** Never wear outdoor shoes inside the Hub or a Japanese home. Use the provided slippers or your socks.
* **Voice Levels:** Keep your voice low in the hallways, on trains, and in public spaces. Silence is a form of respect in Tohoku.
* **Interactions:** Be sensitive to "friendly touching." In Japan, physical boundaries are different than in the West; avoid excessive hugging or physical contact in public.

---

## 3. The "Full Learning Week"
We follow the University of the Nations (UofN) model for outreach. Your week will include:
* **Direct Ministry:** Evangelism, service projects, and church partnership.
* **Intercession:** Group prayer for the people of Sendai and the Tohoku region.
* **Work Duties:** Helping with the daily maintenance, cleaning, and cooking at the Hub.
* **Quiet Time:** We expect 30 minutes of personal prayer and Bible study daily to stay spiritually healthy.

---

## 4. Packing Essentials
* **Church Wear:** "Smart casual" is required. No jeans for church services; opt for slacks or skirts.
* **Water & Nutrition:** Bring a reusable water bottle. Staying hydrated is vital for maintaining your energy during long ministry days.

<Aside type="tip" title="Being a Learner">
The best way to serve Japan is to be a student of the culture. Ask questions, observe carefully, and lead with humility.
</Aside>
