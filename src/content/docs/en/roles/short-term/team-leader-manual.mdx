As a team leader, you are the final authority for your team, serving under the local leadership of YWAM Sendai. Your role is to serve your team by exercising your authority appropriately and managing the overall responsibilities of the outreach.

---

## 1. Initial Logistics & Arrival
* **The 24-Hour Meeting:** Meet with the Sendai Hub leadership within the first 24 hours of arrival to finalize your schedule, including ministry, team times, and days off.
* **Emergency Info:** Ensure you carry two photocopies of every team member's passport and their emergency contact information at all times.
* **Base Orientation:** Arrange for a "Base and Culture" talk for your team on Day 2 or 3 to help them adjust to the Sendai context.

---

## 2. Managing Team Life
* **Daily Leader Meetings:** Meet with your co-leaders every morning or evening to pray, clear up issues, and delegate tasks for the day.
* **The "Little Things":** Watch for small relational tensions or cliques. Confront these early before they become larger problems.
* **One-on-Ones:** You are expected to have a 30-minute check-in with each student every week to provide pastoral support.

---

## 3. Ministry & Cultural Integrity
* **Clothing Standards:** In Japan, modesty is highly valued. Ensure the team follows the clothing guidelines provided for church and ministry settings.
* **Public Witness:** Remind the team to remain quiet and attentive during services, even if they don't understand the language.
* **Hospitality:** If offered food or tea, respond graciously. We avoid refusing hospitality unless absolutely necessary for health reasons.

<Aside type="caution" title="Finances">
Ensure you manage your team budget with integrity. Before leaving the base, reconcile all bills and ensure any local conflict is resolved.
</Aside>
