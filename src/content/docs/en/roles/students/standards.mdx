YWAM Sendai is a program registered with the University of the Nations (UofN). Students receive a grade of either **"Successful" (S)** or **"Unsuccessful" (U)**. To maintain a healthy environment for growth and to receive your certificate, we observe the following standards.

---

## 1. Requirements for Success (S)
A certificate of completion is granted based on the satisfactory fulfillment of these criteria:

*   **Financial Integrity:** Full payment of all school fees.
*   **Academic Diligence:** 100% attendance at lectures, intercession, and work duties. Absence for illness must be reported to the School Leader before 08:00.
*   **Assignments:** Satisfactory completion of all journals, book reports, and projects, submitted by Sunday at 21:00.
*   **Ministry & Outreach:** Participation in any set local ministries and successful completion of outreach phase (if DTS).
*   **Heart Attitude:** Consistent, prompt participation and an attitude of humility and teachability.

---

## 2. Community Rhythms
*   **Work Duties:** Every student is assigned a cleaning, kitchen, or maintenance duty as an act of worship.
*   **Digital Boundaries:** We observe a [Digital Curfew](../../../operations/digital) at 21:00 and Quiet Hours from 22:00 to 07:00.
*   **Single-Heartedness:** During the school, we "fast" from pursuing new romantic relationships to offer our exclusive affection to Christ.

<Aside type="danger" title="Smoking & Alcohol">
Students must refrain from smoking and vaping for the duration of the school. Being drunk with alcohol will result in immediate expulsion.
</Aside>

---

## 3. The Restoration & Expulsion Process (U)
If a student struggles with attitudes or actions that negatively affect the community, we follow a four-step process focused on restoration and accountability.

<Steps>
1.  **Informal Encouragement:** Staff provide consistent encouragement and direct, humble confrontation of issues as they arise.
2.  **Formal Warning:** A planned meeting where the student must verbalize their understanding that their current path leads to failure (U) or expulsion. Together, staff and student work on a solution.
3.  **Final Warning:** A set period is given for a final decision to change behavior. At this point, the school community may be informed in line with the Matthew 18 principle.
4.  **Expulsion/Failure:** If all other avenues are exhausted, the DTS Leader, in consultation with base leadership, will issue an automatic failure or expulsion.
</Steps>

<Aside type="note">
We are not looking for perfection, but for a true desire to be more Christ-like.
</Aside>
