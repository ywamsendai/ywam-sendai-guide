## LEGACY WORD #1: The Vision of the Waves (1956)
**The Foundational Covenant of YWAM**

> "Suddenly I was looking at a map of the world... waves were crashing onto their shores... The waves become young people covering all the continents."

### The Narrative
It was June of 1956. Loren Cunningham was in the Bahamas with four other young men to evangelize and gather young people together using their musical gifts. On a Wednesday at 3 PM, a few days before his twenty-first birthday, he was kneeling by the bed in the simply furnished guest room of his missionary host. He was asking the Lord about the message he was to speak that evening. Then, as he looked up at the white walls, something unexpected happened.

He says, “Suddenly I was looking at a map of the world, only the map was alive and moving! I could see all the continents, and waves were crashing onto their shores. Each wave went onto a continent, then receded, then came up further until it covered the continent completely. The waves become young people – kids my age and even younger – covering all the continents of the globe. They were talking to people on street corners and outside bars. They were going from house to house and preaching the Gospel. They came from everywhere and went everywhere, caring for people. Then just as suddenly as it had come the scene was gone.” (Excerpt from *Is That Really You, God?* by Loren Cunningham with Janice Rogers).

### The Covenantal Elements
* **Youth as the Messenger:** A call to champion young people and lead out apostolically to birth fresh, entrepreneurial initiatives.
* **The "All and Every":** A mandate to be global, comprehensive, and inclusive, reaching every nation and every category of human life.
* **Expanding Waves:** A vision of multi-generational, fractal-like multiplication where each wave builds on the one before.

## LEGACY WORD #2: The Spheres of Influence (1975)
**Discipling Nations through Worldview Transformation**



### The Rocky Mountain Revelation
The phone call was received by the ranger’s station in the Colorado Rockies as the Cunninghams were enjoying a family vacation. Would Loren and Darlene join Bill and Vonette Bright – founders of Campus Crusade – for dinner later that week? Loren eagerly accepted the invitation. He had been asking God for understanding on how to see a nation discipled and God had just spoken to him about **seven influential spheres of society** that shape the worldview, beliefs and values of a culture.

As they met for dinner, Loren had this new understanding scrawled on a yellow sheet from a legal pad. Before he could share, Bill blurted out, “Loren, you won’t believe what God has just shown me. If we are going to see our nations changed, we have to impact seven different spheres of society....” Within a month, Francis Schaeffer also spoke of these same seven areas.

### The Seven Spheres
These seven spheres exist in every society and include:
1. **Family**
2. **Economy** (Business/Science)
3. **Government**
4. **Religion**
5. **Education**
6. **Media** (Communication)
7. **Celebration** (Arts/Entertainment/Sports)

The goal is to bring transformational change to nations by intentionally applying kingdom principles in each of these arenas (*Coram Deo*).

## LEGACY WORD #3: The Christian Magna Carta (1981)
**The Fundamental Gospel Rights of Every Human Being**

### The Narrative
Late in 1981, YWAM leaders gathered in Kona, Hawaii. Loren sensed God speaking to him about what he understood to be the "Christian Magna Carta." Just as the original Magna Carta (1215) detailed human rights, this document details the **Gospel rights** that every human being has.

### The Six Points
Everyone on earth has the right to:
1. **Hear and understand** the Gospel of Jesus Christ.
2. **Have a Bible available** in his/her own language.
3. **Have a Christian fellowship** available nearby.
4. **Have a Biblical Christian education** available for their children.
5. **Have the basic necessities of life:** food, water, clothing, shelter, and health care.
6. **Lead a productive life of fulfillment** spiritually, mentally, socially, emotionally, and physically.

## LEGACY WORD #4: End Bible Poverty (1967 / 2014)
**The Vision of the Bible Truck**

### The Narrative
In 1967, while in Mexico, a woman in a faded red dress asked Loren, “Do you have a Bible in my language?” Later, Loren saw a vision of a large moving van with the words: *“Sólo los deshonestos temen la verdad. Santa Biblia, gratis”* (Only the dishonest fear the truth. Free Bibles).

### The Challenge
In 2003, and again in 2014, this vision culminated in a formal commitment: **"The Covenant to End Bible Poverty."**
* **The Goal:** To put a Bible in every home in the world in their heart language.
* **The Action:** Calling on Christians everywhere to pray, translate, publish, distribute, educate, and motivate people for Bible engagement.
