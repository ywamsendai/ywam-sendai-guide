These six covenants represent the formal "stakes in the ground" where the YWAM family has gathered to repent, realign, and commit to new apostolic directions.

---

## 1. The Manila Covenant (1988)
**A Statement of Mission and Affirmation**

The Manila Covenant was confirmed by 1,500 staff in the Philippines. It serves as a comprehensive declaration of YWAM's identity and calling.

### The 20 Affirmations
We affirm:
* **The Great Commission:** Our calling is to help complete the Great Commission through evangelism, training, and mercy.
* **Mobilizing Youth:** Giving young people leadership roles and influence.
* **The Unreached:** Reaching the 9 frontiers (Muslim, Buddhist, Hindu, Communist, Children, Nominal Christians, Cities, Poor, and Tribal).
* **Lordship of Christ:** Proclaiming Jesus over all spheres of society (Family, Church, Education, Media, Arts, Commerce, Science, Government).
* **International Diversity:** Staff and leadership representative of all nations and ethnicities.
* **Word and Deed:** Proclaiming the Gospel through both personal evangelism and practical acts of mercy.
* **God's Way:** Rejecting paternalism, prejudice, and triumphalism.
* **Gender and Age Equality:** Both young and old, male and female in leadership.
* **Servant Leadership:** Accountability and a lifestyle of righteous humility.
* **Unity:** Commitment to transparency and the principles of Ephesians 4 and 5.
* **Biblical Balance:** Valuing Christians of all theological backgrounds.
* **The Local Church:** Working as partners to multiply and build up local churches.
* **Intercession:** Building everything on the foundation of prayer.
* **Accountability:** Welcoming correction and openness within our corporate and personal lives.
* **Individual Value:** Empowering every person God sends to us.
* **Hospitality:** Opening our bases, homes, and hearts.
* **Financial Integrity:** Living by the highest spiritual and ethical standards.
* **Purpose and Belief:** (Affirming the Statement of Purpose and the Christian Magna Carta).

## 2. The Red Sea Covenant (1992)
**Commitment to the Muslim World**

In April 1992, the Lord spoke forcefully about YWAM's need for deeper involvement in the Muslim world and a "driving of a stake" to claim God's direction.

### Our Resolve
We solemnly resolve before God that we will:
* **Pursue New Anointing:** Actively seek the enabling power God wants to pour out.
* **Spiritual Discipline:** Submit to fasting, prayer, and repentance.
* **Whole Vision:** Seeing both Jews and Arabs as God's beloved creation, refusing to participate in bitterness.
* **Muslim World Focus:** Seeking strategies to reach those under the influence of Islam.
* **Mobilization:** Exercising leadership to reach Muslim peoples globally.

## 3. The Nanning Covenant (2002)
**Commitment to Apostolic Pioneering and Eldership**

Gathered in Nanning, China, the Global Leadership Team renewed their commitment to the Lordship of Jesus and the "Project 4K" challenge.

### Key Commitments
* **Project 4K:** Reaffirming the next challenge of apostolic pioneering.
* **Freedom in the Spirit:** Releasing the newest to the oldest YWAMers to obey God's voice.
* **Spiritual Eldership:** Submitting ministries to spiritual eldership and healthy coaching.
* **Transparency:** Fresh emphasis on God's Word as our compass for daily living.

## 4. The Jubilee Covenant (2010)
**Personal Commitment to the Next 50 Years**

As YWAM celebrated 50 years, this covenant was designed for personal commitment to ensure "the flame goes forward."

### The 5 Commitments
1. **Love God and Neighbor** (Mark 12:28-31).
2. **Seek First the Kingdom** above personal concerns.
3. **Serve Others** out of love for God.
4. **Take up the Cross** and follow Jesus anywhere.
5. **Fulfill the Great Commission** in this generation.

## 5. The Singapore Covenant (2014)
**Renewal of the Vision of the Waves**

This covenant serves as a historical realignment, acknowledging where YWAM had drifted into "Saul's Armor" (organizational hierarchy) and returning to a "Family" model.

### The Realignment (The Tripod Message)
We emphasize three essential pillars:
1. **Individual Freedom:** The freedom to hear and obey God in a co-creative way.
2. **Spiritual Eldership:** Elders who serve and convene rather than guard positions.
3. **Healthy Relationships:** Loving relationships under the Lordship of Jesus.

### Core Focus
* **Championing Youth:** Recommitting to the deregulation of missions.
* **The Alls and Everys:** Going where we are not, reaching every person on earth.
* **Altars of Stone:** Building relationships and unity rather than "towers of bricks" (hierarchies).

## 6. A Covenant to End Bible Poverty (2014)
**Commitment to Scripture Accessibility**

A pledge to collaborate with the Body of Christ to ensure everyone has meaningful access to God's Word.

### The Five-Fold Action Plan
* **Translate:** Faciliate translation into every remaining language by 2033.
* **Publish:** Use all methods (print, digital, audio, video) to reach every learning style.
* **Distribute:** Ensure every household on earth has the opportunity to have a Bible.
* **Educate:** Equip people to read, hear, and apply the Word of God.
* **Motivate:** Birth a movement of frequent and regular Bible engagement.
