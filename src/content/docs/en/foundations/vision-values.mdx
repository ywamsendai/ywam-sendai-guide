# Vision & Values: The Tohoku Charter

YWAM Sendai is a modern monastic-missional community. Our primary calling is to **Know God and to Make Him Known**. We believe that the most effective way to reach Japan is through a life deeply hidden in Christ, where our own transformation becomes the light that draws others to Him.

---

## 1. Our Identity: A Mission Hub
Since our pioneering in 2018, YWAM Sendai has served as the **Mission Hub for the Tohoku region**. We are more than a single base; we are a spiritual engine room designed to fuel the expansion of the Kingdom across every prefecture in North Japan.

As a **Training Center**, our primary work is the formation of the missionary. Through the Discipleship Training School (DTS) and secondary schools, we offer a space where believers are not only equipped with ministry skills but are invited into a transformative "walk with God" that is deep, resilient, and rooted.

---

## 2. The Tohoku Mandate: Every Prefecture
Our apostolic mandate is clear: **to see a vibrant YWAM presence and ministry team established in every one of the six prefectures of Tohoku.** Sendai stands as the "Mother Base," providing the resources, training, and spiritual oversight necessary to pioneer new teams. We believe that Tohoku will be reached by a "constellation" of small, faithful communities rooted in local soil.

---

## 3. Our Heart: The Three Pillars
To sustain this regional mandate, we live by three core convictions that define our "Local Flavor":

*   **Intimacy as Mission (Theosis):** We believe mission flows out of *being* before it flows out of *doing*. Our goal is theosis—the lifelong journey of being transformed into the likeness of God. When the beauty of Christ is reflected in a transformed life, the Gospel becomes compelling to the Japanese soul.
*   **Deep Contextualization:** For the Gospel to truly take root in Japanese soil, it must find a native expression. We value the "slow mission" of language fluency, the aesthetic of beauty, and presenting Christianity as an ancient "Way" that has been part of the Japanese story for centuries.
*   **Faithful Longevity:** We value the beauty of the long-term journey. We view our health, communal rhythms, and relationships as sacred stewardship for a marathon of faith.

---

## 4. Our Foundation: The House of Unity
YWAM Sendai is a home for believers from all major Christian streams. Our unity is built on a robust foundation:

*   **The Nicene Creed:** The shared confession of the historic, apostolic faith.
*   **YWAM Foundational Values:** The guiding principles of our global movement.
*   **Mutual Honor:** A culture that respects the sacramental integrity of each person's tradition while laboring together for the local church in Tohoku.

---

## 5. The Rule of Life: How We Walk
We anchor our vision in a shared **Rule of Life**. We recognize that a missionary's "doing" must flow from their "being." We commit ourselves to a life anchored in common prayer, the study of the Word, and walking in accountability with our leaders and local church expressions.
