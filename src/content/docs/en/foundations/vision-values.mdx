YWAM Sendai is a **modern monastic-missional community**. Our primary calling is to **Know God and to Make Him Known**. We believe the most effective way to reach Japan is through a life deeply hidden in Christ, where personal transformation becomes the light that draws others to Him.

---

## 1. Our Identity: The Mission Hub for Tohoku
Since its pioneering in 2018, YWAM Sendai has served as the **Mission Hub for the Tohoku region (North Japan)**. We function as a spiritual "engine room" designed to fuel the expansion of the Kingdom across all six prefectures in the region.

**Our Role as a Training Center:**
Our primary work is the formation of the missionary. Through the Discipleship Training School (DTS) and secondary schools, we offer a space for transformative spiritual formation that is deep, resilient, and rooted in the Japanese context.

---

## 2. The Tohoku Mandate: Six Prefectures
Our apostolic mandate is to see a vibrant YWAM presence and ministry team established in every one of the **six prefectures of Tohoku** (Aomori, Iwate, Miyagi, Akita, Yamagata, and Fukushima). 

* **Sendai as Mission Hub:** We provide resources, training, and spiritual oversight for pioneering.
* **The Movement:** We reach Tohoku through a network of small, faithful communities rooted in local soil rather than one centralized institution.

---

## 3. The Three Pillars of our "Local Flavor"
To sustain our regional mandate in Japan, we live by three core convictions:

1. **Intimacy as Mission (Theosis):** Mission flows out of *being* before *doing*. We pursue theosis—the lifelong journey of being transformed into the likeness of God.
2. **Deep Contextualization:** For the Gospel to take root in Japanese soil, it must find a native expression. We value language fluency, aesthetic beauty, and presenting Christianity as an ancient "Way" for the Japanese people.
3. **Faithful Longevity:** We view our health, communal rhythms, and relationships as sacred stewardship for a "marathon of faith."

---

## 4. The Theological Foundation: The House of Unity
YWAM Sendai is an interdenominational home built on a robust foundation:

* **The Nicene Creed:** Our shared confession of the historic, apostolic faith.
* **YWAM Foundational Values:** The 18 guiding principles of our global movement.
* **Mutual Honor:** Respecting the sacramental integrity of various Christian traditions while serving the local church in Tohoku.

---

## 5. The Rule of Life: Rhythms of Grace
We anchor our vision in a shared **Rule of Life**. We recognize that a missionary's "doing" must flow from their "being." 

**Our Daily Commitments:**
* **Common Prayer:** Regular times of gathering for intercession and worship.
* **Study of the Word:** Deep engagement with the Bible as our textbook for life.
* **Accountability:** Walking in relationship with leaders and local church expressions.
