The story of YWAM Sendai is one of persistent prayer, patient waiting, and the slow work of becoming rooted in the soil of Tohoku. 

---

## 2010 – 2018: The Years of Vision
Before there was a permanent presence in Tohoku, there was prayer. From 2010 to 2018, Gary and Naoko Seddon—then staffing with YWAM Tokyo—led numerous vision trips, prayer walks, and outreach teams across the various prefectures of the region. 

During these years, the lack of a long-term YWAM presence in the north of Japan became a heavy burden on their hearts. Through many visits, it became clear that God was highlighting **Sendai** as the gateway and the starting point for YWAM’s work in the region.

---

## 2018: The Pioneering Seed
In the summer of 2018, the vision moved from scouting to pioneering. The Seddons, along with another couple, were officially sent out from YWAM Tokyo to establish a community in Sendai. 

Like any pioneering work, these early days were about laying foundations—learning the neighborhood, building local relationships, and establishing the spiritual "hearth" of the base.

---

## 2020 – 2023: Waiting and Equipping
Just as the team began to grow, the global COVID-19 pandemic arrived. This period was a significant "Selah" for the community—a time of waiting and hiddenness. 

While many activities were paused, the team used these years for internal equipping, deepening their language skills, and strengthening the relational bonds of the growing community. It was during this time that our identity as a modern monastic-missional community began to take deep root.

---

## 2023 – Present: The Season of Fruit
As borders reopened and the "waiting" season ended, the community transitioned into a season of training and multiplication.

* **September 2023:** We successfully ran our first bilingual Discipleship Training School (DTS).
* **September 2024:** We launched our second bilingual DTS.
* **Today:** We are now a diverse family of approximately 12 full-time members, representing Japan, the UK, Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Singapore, the Philippines, and the United States.

---

## Looking Forward
Our journey continues as we look toward new milestones in our mission to Tohoku:
* **September 2026:** Launch of our third bilingual DTS.
* **Spring 2027:** Launch of our first Discipleship Bible School (DBS).

We believe our history is still being written. Every staff member, student, and volunteer who joins us becomes a part of this unfolding story of God’s faithfulness in Sendai.
</Aside>
