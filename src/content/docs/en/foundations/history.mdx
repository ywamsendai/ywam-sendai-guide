## 2010 – 2018: The Years of Vision
Before there was a permanent presence in Tohoku, there was prayer. Gary and Naoko Seddon—then staffing with YWAM Tokyo—led numerous vision trips, prayer walks, and outreach teams across the region. 

During these years, God highlighted **Sendai** as the strategic gateway. The lack of a long-term YWAM presence in Northern Japan became a burden that eventually led to the official pioneering call.

## 2018: The Pioneering Seed
In the summer of 2018, the Seddons and another couple were officially sent out from YWAM Tokyo. These early days were focused on:
* **Relational Foundations:** Building trust within the local Sendai neighborhood.
* **Spiritual Hearth:** Establishing the daily rhythms of prayer and worship that define the base today.

## 2020 – 2023: The "Selah" Season
The COVID-19 pandemic served as a season of hiddenness and preparation. This period was critical for:
* **Equipping:** Internal training and intensive language study.
* **Identity:** Our identity as a **modern monastic-missional community** took root, emphasizing depth of relationship and spiritual discipline over busyness.

## 2023 – Present: Training and Multiplication
The end of the "waiting" season marked a transition into fruitfulness and formal training.

<Steps>
1. **September 2023:** Successfully ran the first bilingual Discipleship Training School (DTS).
2. **September 2024:** Launched the second bilingual DTS.
3. **Current Season:** Transitioning into a diverse family of 12 full-time members representing 8 nations.
</Steps>

## Future Milestones
We are moving toward a future of deeper biblical training and regional impact:
* **September 2026:** Launch of the third bilingual DTS.
* **Spring 2027:** Launch of our first **Discipleship Bible School (DBS)**.
